Simplify Cookies Configuration

Easily setup cookie notice that compliant with all major privacy regulations and frameworks

Team: Product Manager, Tech Leads @ OneTrust

Duration: Nov 2019 - Dec 2019

Key Words: Enterprise UX, Desktop Design

My Role: UX Design, Usability Testing

Overview

Project Background

To comply with¬†global privacy regulations, including GDPR from EU and CCPA from California, US, website owners need to provide notice about the cookies on a website before they are dropped, and need to provide controls of the cookies to site visitors. While the privacy landscape taking over the entire world, an increasing number of organizations of all sizes are using our cookie product to help them prepare for these new laws.

Problem

The current product was originally designed for huge companies with a team of technical and legal persons to work with. For most marketers and small business owners who have neither technical nor legal background, the current product is bulky and difficult to use. They have to invest an extra amount of time learning to use the product. Therefore, a better tool is in need to enable a more intuitive implementation process.

Design Outcomes
Setup Wizard

Step by step guides users through the key steps of configurations. Use a live preview to give users real-time feedback on their selections.

Advanced Container

Bring all necessary configurations together for users to bulk manage a group of websites in one place. Allow users to override at the website level as well.

Research

Even though I was given the prompt, I needed to break down the project space into achievable tasks and tackle them through a solid design process as well as rigorous collaboration.

Defining User Needs

At the outset of the project, I didn't have a clear goal but abstract ideas from product managers. To better understand users‚Äô needs, I scheduled observation sessions as well as focus group discussions with clients of different sizes to answer the following questions.

Small business owners and marketing persons are the primary users
Users simply want to set up quickly and be compliant
The current product is bulky and difficult to use

After I talked with clients and customer supports, I found people's frustrations generally fell into the following three categories:

1. ‚ÄúI have to go everywhere to change everything.‚Äù

Clients complained that even the current tool supports all the functionalities, it's hard to use because all pieces are separate. Even they just want to make a small change like changing button color, they have to go to multiple places to make the change appear on their websites.

2. ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt know what‚Äôs the next step. How can I put the banner I just created on my website?‚Äù

Clients were lost in the configuration process because we didn't provide enough information around where the functions are located and what actions are required to finish the configuration. It's like throwing users into a forest and there is no way out.

3. "I need to be compliant but I know nothing about these privacy laws. What kind of configuration settings should I use?"

Most clients don't have a background in privacy regulations, so it's difficult for them to know what settings are required. And our tool didn't inform users appropriately. Even if some clients have a privacy team in their organizations to consult, it will cause a lot of back and forth.

Current User Flow

To better understand what blocks users from achieving their goals, I audit the current design and found the gaps from the current user flow.

Design Goals

Based on the user needs and the gaps in existing products, I summarized the higher-level goals that I hope to achieve with the redesign.

Design

Proposed New User Flow

The ideation starts with a high-level user flowchart to collect feedback from stakeholders, including developers and product managers, this has been working well for me to inform the team on my design processes and raise UX awareness for the whole team.

User Feedbacks & Revised Design Goals

All users liked the idea of having a centralized place to manage all configurations and keep the advanced settings at the current locations. This was reassuring for us that the redesign brings value to users. We also received suggestions that added more to our design goals.

- Explain the concept of "Container" at the very beginning

It‚Äôs too overwhelming to ask users to make the decisions of containers at the very beginning, especially for new users who are not familiar with the concept of a container.

- Add a Setup Wizard

Based on feedback, I created a refined user flow and added a setup wizard in order to educate new users on the concept of "Container". The new flow got great feeback from stakeholders.

Design Alternatives

After a few iterations, I created the high-fidelity interface to illustrate primary functionalities and gather further feedbacks from clients.

  • Moved the progress bar to the top, and make the preview more useful.

Evaluation

Through several review sessions with clients, we received positive feedback for the design solutions. There were also suggestions that we can make the whole process more transparent and understandable. And at the end of the wizard, we can make the transition to advanced container smoother.

Enhancement #1: Use a loading animation to inform users what‚Äôs happening in the backend
Enhancement #2: Prompt users for the next step. And allow users to share with their privacy team for review.

Reflections

This is a problem that I've been wishing to solve for a long time. Since I noticed that we have been focused on individual features too much and lost the big picture of the overall workflow. Through multiple iterations, we have arrived at a good solution for now. Two lessons I learned from it are:

Ask for feedbacks but don't let users make design decisions.

We received feedbacks from different stakeholders, like clients, sales teams, customer support teams. All this information allows me to understand the pains and frustrations users experience as they use our product and see problems I might have otherwise missed. However, I can't just take the feedbacks and design a solution based on what users said. I need to dig deeper to figure out users' problems and interpret them to design solutions.

Define design scope at an early stage.

We had a high expectation about this project before started, however it exposed to tons of limitations during the design process. Reasonably planning the design scope of each stage will save time, energy, and eliminate distractions. It will also involve developers in the design process at an early stage to make sure the design is feasible.
